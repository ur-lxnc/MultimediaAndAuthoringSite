Rotoscoping
Project

Rotoscoping is a classical animation technique in which animators trace over live-action footage frame by frame to create smooth, lifelike movement. Originally developed for traditional cel animation, it has evolved into a powerful digital technique used in modern film, music videos, and interactive media. By extracting and reinterpreting motion from real subjects, rotoscoping bridges the gap between reality and illustration.

Process

Step-by-Step Tutorial

A detailed walkthrough of the rotoscoping workflow — from initial setup to final export. Each step includes a supporting tutorial video clip.

01
Prepare the Applications
Install and configure Adobe After Effects and Adobe Photoshop. Set up your project workspace, import the source footage, and ensure all necessary plugins and export presets are ready before beginning the tracing process.
Video — Step 1
02
Split the Video into 5-Second Clips
Divide the source footage into manageable 5-second segments. Working in shorter clips makes the rotoscoping process more precise and reduces errors when tracing motion across many frames.
Video — Step 2
03
Open After Effects — Export via Render Queue
Import your footage into Adobe After Effects. Use the Roto Brush tool or manual masking to trace the subject frame by frame. Once complete, go to Composition → Add to Render Queue and export the rendered frames as an image sequence.
Video — Step 3
04
Import Rendered Frames into Photoshop
After rendering is complete, open Adobe Photoshop and import the exported image sequence. Photoshop's Timeline panel allows you to view and work with each frame individually for detailed cleanup and artistic editing.
Video — Step 4
05
Edit the Rendered Frames
With all frames imported, apply your artistic style — adjust colors, clean up edges, add effects, or stylize the traced figures. Once all frames are polished, export the final animation as a video file for the complete rotoscoped output.
Video — Step 5
